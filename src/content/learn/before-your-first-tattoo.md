# Before your first tattoo

Most first-timers underestimate how long the day will feel. A small piece can take 60-90 minutes; a half-sleeve can run 4-6 hours. The right prep keeps you comfortable and makes for a cleaner result.

## The day before

- Sleep well. A tired body bruises and bleeds more.
- Hydrate. Skin holds ink better when it isn't dehydrated.
- Skip alcohol for 24 hours. It thins your blood.
- Don't get sunburned in the area being tattooed.

## What to wear

- Loose clothing that exposes the placement easily.
- Dark colors — ink stains.
- Layers, since shops are often cooler than expected.

## During the session

- Eat a proper meal an hour beforehand.
- Bring water, snacks, and headphones for long sessions.
- Tell your artist if you need a break. They'd rather you tap out than push through and tense up.

## What to ask your artist

- How long will the session run?
- What does aftercare look like for this style and placement?
- When should I expect my first touch-up?

The artist's chair is collaborative — speak up about pain, comfort, and design changes early.
