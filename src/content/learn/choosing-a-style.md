# Choosing a tattoo style

Style isn't decoration — it shapes how a piece holds up over years. Use this as a starting map.

## Traditional

Bold lines, limited color palette, classic motifs (anchors, roses, eagles). Ages exceptionally well.

## Neo-Traditional

A descendant of Traditional with finer lines and a wider palette. Stands the test of time, but requires touch-ups sooner than Traditional.

## Japanese (Irezumi)

Large-scale, narrative compositions — dragons, koi, hannya. Built for long sessions and strong contrast. Ages beautifully on bodies.

## Realism

Photoreal portraiture. Flattering up close, hard to maintain — fine detail blurs over time, especially in color.

## Fine Line / Minimalist

Delicate, precise, often single-needle. Stunning fresh, but the most likely style to fade or blur within 5-10 years.

## Blackwork / Tribal

Heavy black ink, geometric or solid fills. The most age-resistant family of styles.

## Watercolor

Soft gradients without bold outlines. High visual impact but the fastest to fade.

## How to pick

- Match style to placement. Fine line on a finger ≠ same investment as fine line on a forearm.
- Match style to maintenance tolerance. Are you OK with touch-ups every 3 years?
- Match style to artist. Each artist has 2-3 styles they truly excel at — the [Style DNA](/app/artist) on a profile shows you which ones.
